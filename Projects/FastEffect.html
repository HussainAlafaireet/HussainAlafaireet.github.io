AR Presenter

AR presentation for the chaotic trade show environment


View Process   View Final Output

Project Overview

The typical AR app performs badly in the trade show environment.

Roles: Project Lead, UX Researcher,Product Designer  |  Duration: 8 Weeks
Tools Used: Unity (initial prototyping, networking), Hololens Toolkit (connecting Hololens to Unity), Microsoft Sharing Services (used to establish app spatial tracking)
UX Skills Used: User/Stakeholder Interviews, Personas, Storyboarding, Prototyping , Stakeholder Pitching

Summary: AR Presenter, developed by Fast Effect in Summer 2018, is a Hololens-based Augmented Reality software used to demonstrate products in trade/tech show venues. AR Presenter uses a careful combination of local-area location sharing, simplified presenter control, and modular AR presentation construction to defeat the the problems typical AR products face when used in the show environment.

My Contributions:

As Project Lead, I drove the vision and timetable for the 2-month development of the initial AR Presenter prototype.

As UX Researcher, I conducted stakeholder research, affinity wall analysis, and persona construction to determine true user needs.

As Product Designer, I generated the feature list, user journey, and interaction design for AR Presenter.

Process


Explore

I worked on behalf of Fast Effect with one of their pre-existing clients, a car technology manufacturer in the Detroit, MI area. The client had not been satisfied with the their previous attempts at AR-enhanced product demonstration product demonstration app, and their Hololens units had been underutilized ever since.

My task was to determine the reasons for the dissatisfaction, understand where the client wanted to next head with Augmented Reality, and design a solution for that new direction.

Stakeholder Interviews

I began my research by conducting a set of semi-structured stakeholder interviews. I interviewed the heads of the two client departments using the previous app: the head of R&D marketing, and the head of community outreach. With permission from the client, the interview's audio was recorded.

Previous App Review

I also conducted an Lessons Learned review of the previous app with the Fast Effect development team. The development team was able to provide technical insights into the performance of the previous product demonstration app.

Analysis


Affinity Wall

Once the exploration activities were complete, I introduced Fast Effect to the concepts of interpretation sessions and affinity walls. During the interpretation session, I led a facilitated review of the audio recordings pulling on my UX experience and the team's institutional knowledge of the client to identify important points. We then conducted an affinity wall session to organize and categorize these key points, deriving 4 key findings.



Analysis Findings


1. The app experience had to work with minimal AR expertise

  • The client's end users had minimal AR app experience.
  • High visitor throughput left no time to train even basic app use.


2. The client app should adapt to venue space with minimal setup effort

  • Trade show environment presents technical issues for Hololens apps
    • Poor lighting
    • Limited Wireless Access
    • Traffic flow disrupting spatial tracking
  • Client reps didn't have the expertise to troubleshoot such challenges


3. Long-term app success required consideration of multiple use cases

  • Client org transitioning from manufacturing to technology firm.
  • AR used for both technical product demonstration and student outreach.
  • Client technical experts skeptical about the use of AR.


4. The client app must be capable of telling compelling stories

  • Client wanted to portray itself as forward-thinking.
  • Client wanted AR technology to "wow and dazzle" visitors
  • Client was positive to the notion of "building" a presentation out of "car parts"
Based on our research insights, I developed four design principles to guide further design and development.

AR Presenter Design Principles


Use only proven AR tracking technologies, and avoid room-wide spacial mapping

Ensure that the app can adapt to a variety of venue formats.



Tailor the prototype to a specific client message

Build a modular storytelling approach into the app.

Personas

I next developed personas based on our two known client users: trade show presenters and outreach presenters.

These personas were then used to ground all future brainstorming sessions, and keep everyday focus on our users.

Ideation

Team Brainstorming

I facilitated a brainstorming session with the technical team to develop the core concept for our new app. the central design idea of the new app. We used the research findings and personas to guide our brainstorming.

The most applicable concept generated was a guided presenter experience using Hololens AR to enhance a physical presenter.

Design

The AR Presenter design concept was a modular presentation app, with powerful, adaptive storytelling front and center.

Interaction Design

I next built out the interaction design for the application. I used an set of interaction design sketches to outline the main interaction areas of the application:

AR Presenter Features


Adaptive Location Selection

In order to navigate a potentially confusing trade/tech show environment, the presenter has the option of placing a "marker" hologram to designate the physical location that best suits the presentation at hand.


Controlled Presenter Experience

While in presentation mode, the presenter has full control over the experience, with the ability to switch between pieces of AR content, and the ability to trigger specific interactions on AR content, which are then reflected in the presentee view in real-time.


Voice Chat

Trade and Tech Show environments can be quite loud. AR Presenter implements voice chat through the Hololens microphone and speaker system to compensate for the noise.


Standalone Mode

In situations where a presenter isn't available, presentations can be run in a "standalone" mode, with presentees able to use simplified versions of the presenter controls to walk through the experience.


"Parts" Ecosystem

The presentation system is modular. The AR Presenteer app has a dedicated mode for building presentations, utilizing a library of AR content that can be expanded as needed.


"Safe" Shared Experience Environment

AR Presenter utilizes a sharing experience revolving around a local wireless router packaged with the system, married to a single shared anchor placed when the presentation is started. This ensures a usable shared experience even in crowded venue spaces with poor lighting conditions.


Lo-Fi Prototyping

I also developed a series of Lo-Fi Photoshop prototype shots for use in a client pitch presentation.

Client Pitch Presentation

I pitched the AR Presenter concept directly to our client, using a combination of our research results, design concepts, and development plans.

The client was pleased with the AR Presenter concept, and authorized the creation of an initial prototype.

Develop

Sprint Planning

I planned the two-week sprint requiried to develop the initial prototype. This spring had the following planning elements:

  • Breakdown of tasks between graphic design, 3-D Art, and Development
  • A triaged list of features that struck a good balance between concept demonstration and development effort.
  • A use case scenario that included both a close-up AR car view and an overhead road view.
  • A detailed user journey map to aid the transition from design to development

Once I had developed a plan, I held a sprint kick-off meeting with the technical team to present the client feedback and sprint plan. Having this formal meeting ensured that all members of the technical team were on the same page, and gave them a venue to check the feasibility of the sprint plan and ask for any additional resources they needed.

Final Output

Demo

Client Walkthrough

At the end of the second week of sprints, we invited our two client stakeholders in to test our demo. We walked the client through our demo scenario. We then encouraged them to experiment with the menu system, voice chat, and shared AR object control.

The client was impressed with the demo's capabilities, and authorized the creation of a full version of AR Presenter.

Reflect

Post-Demo Analysis

After the demo presentation, the team assessed the lessons learned from both the development and demonstration processes:



Shared Presenter/Presentee Functionality

The Shared Presenter/Presentee functionality clearly communicated the power and vision of AR Presenter's trade show experience.

Menu Design Offset and Sizing

The presentation layer's sizing, visual design, and positioning proved to be both usable and pleasing.

"Wow and Dazzle" Visual Fidelity

The client was impressed by the immersive factor of our demo scenario. AR Presenter proved its power to tell compelling narratives.


Attention Direction

The client had difficulty knowing precisely where in the road scenario they should be looking at a particular time. Future scenarios would need to be much more clear as to where the "action" was happening.

Left-Handed Presenter Controls

The client pointed out that left-handed users might be less comfortable with the right-positioned presentation controls. We would need to offer a left-aligned menu as an option.

"Scalable Holograms"

The client wanted to have the presentation holograms be scalable, so that they could be adapted to the presentation area in use.
