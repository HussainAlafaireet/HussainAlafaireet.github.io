Mandala Ball

ADHD Neurofeedback Training using Mind over Matter


View Process   View Final Output

Project Overview

Can VR be used to control ADHD?

Role: UX Designer, VR Developer, Team Lead  |  Duration: 6 Weeks
Tools Used: Microsoft Mixed Reality Toolkit, Unity, Oculus Integration Kit
UX Skills Used: Crazy-8 Brainstorming, UX Design, VR Prototyping

Summary: Mandala Ball is a VR medical therapy game for the Oculus Quest. The game trains children with ADHD to manage their ADHD symptoms. Mandala Ball is built around the idea of the “Mind has Power”. Mandala Ball simulates “mind powers” though handtracking input. By tying the use of these “powers” to control of ADHD, we motivate our players to actively manage their ADHD condition.

This project was an entry to the Virtual Medicine Conference 2020 Online Hackathon.

My Contributions:

As Team Lead, I managed a remote team of video producers, medical researchers, and 3-D artists towards a single vision of VR therapy. I acted as the team lead, primary UX designer, and primary developer for Mandala Ball.

As UX Designer, I generated the primary design concept for Mandala Ball, including the Mandala Ball social companion, the key stakeholders, the primary features, and the technical architecture.

As VR Developer, I coded the Mandala Ball VR app, using a combination of Unity, Microsoft Mixed Reality Toolkit, and custom game code.

Process


Explore

Brainstorming

I worked with Vi Li, Jose Rosero, and Eric Tao on this project. As a Hackathon team, our first task was to determine a scope and a core idea that we could build a VR app around. Our initial meeting establish two scoping parameters:

  • Hand Tracking: We were interested in using hand tracking technology to replace controllers in VR apps.
  • Pediatric Care: As a team, we were most interested in supporting pediatric patients, as we felt an emotional connection towards helping them.

Once we had our initial areas of interest, we spent the next week brainstorming ideas. The core idea we rallied around was a VR neurofeedback training game to control ADHD. In ADHD neurofeedback training, users attempt to maintain concentration and focus, and receive real-time feedback in the form of EEG brain measurements. By engaging in this feedback loop, users learn to control their brain, and their ADHD symptoms.

Our game would add VR handtracking elements to simulate telekinetic superpowers. In this way, we would create an interactive, motivating ADHD training game that our target audience would be delighted to play.

Investigate

Secondary Research

To flesh out our core idea, we conducted secondary research on pediatric ADHD, neurofeedback training, and the use of VR to treat ADHD. This research included an academic paper review, a comparative analysis of existing neurofeedback training products, and an interview with an elementary school teacher with ADHD training.

Our research review led to the following insights:

  1. Our design needed to include school-related settings, but with an approach that did not trigger existing social stigma concerns our players might have.
  2. Some level of social interaction would be needed to guide, praise, and encourage our players.
  3. Listening, reading, and taking turn activities would be particularly difficult tasks for our players.
  4. Most tasks should be small, with plenty of room for breaks and task switching.
  5. Therapists and doctors would need a way to monitor training progression over time.

Initial Prototype

To establish a base development pipeline, I used the MRTK-Quest handtracking kit and Unity to create a prototype of our game. This version included:

  • A "concentration sphere" that would be the visual indicator for EEG data. While no EEG data was involved in this build, I provided a vision of the indicator we might use through the use of a color change and a sound to indicate good concentration.
  • An example "telekinesis" activity involving the placement of a coffee cup in a box.
  • A basic living room environment (sourced from Google Poly) with a distracting kitchen background sound.

Design

It was now time to expand out the core idea into a true vision of the game. I synthesized our brainstorming and secondary research to develop "Mandala Ball" - an VR app that uses the idea of "The Mind Has Power" to create an empowering tool for children with ADHD.

The Mandala Ball design concept has the following 5 main features:

Mandala Ball Companion

The Mandala Ball is the central design feature of our app. Inspired by the mandala meditation diagrams from Eastern religion , the Mandala Ball is an ever-present companion. Mandala Ball interact with the player in two ways:

  • talking to the player, providing instructions and encouragement.
  • glowing to indicate player concentration. The better the player concentrates, the more intense the glow.


Mandala Ball Concentration Glow

Session Activities

Mandala Ball activities come in two types: Concentration and Telekinesis

Concentration activities are neurofeedback exercises managed by the Mandala Ball. As the player concentrates, EEG output is fed to the Mandala Ball. The Ball then begins glowing and providing encouragement to promote further concentration.

Concentration Exercise

Telekinesis activities ask the player to complete manipulation tasks via hand-tracking.

Telekinesis exercise

Scaling Environments

Mandala Ball comes with 3 virtual environments: a living room, a school hallway, and a school classroom. These environments were selected for their relevance to everyday ADHD challenges our target audience would face.

In addition to the session settings, each environment has it's own set of audio/visual distractions that can be added for additional challenge.

Simulation Control User Interface

Parents, therapist, and doctors can monitor and control Mandala Ball sessions via a real-time video feed and pre-session setup controls.

Data Analytics Dashboard

Mandala Ball comes with a Data Analytics Dashboard that allows all stakeholders to see progression of VR therapy. Each stakeholder receives a visualization that matches their specific needs.

Develop

Due to time constraints, our Hackathon demo would not include all features from the Mandala Ball design concept. For our demo, we implemented:

  • "Telekinesis" Hand Tracking
  • A prototype Version of the Mandala Ball Companion, using visual design, Unity animations, and voice acting.
  • A prototype training session, including both a Concentration and Telekinesis activity. While direct EEG integration was not part of this demo, the visual indicators used to give feedback were present in the Mandala Ball companion.

During a 1.5 week sprint, I coded the game in Unity. I used Simon Says as the inspiration for our Telekinesis activity, integrating the visual assets produced by the team's technical artist.

Reflect

Short Timeframe - Compelling Vision

With a combination of technical pre-work, professional video construction, and custom coding, we were able to create a compelling vision of need, and how our product would solve that need.

Successful Design Synthesis

I was able to synthesize our secondary research and brainstorming into a unified design concept, and correctly communicate this concept to the rest of my team. This communication unleashed my teammates' potential, by giving them a framework to built on.

Handtracking Technical Development

Thanks to this project, I now have experience with MRTK-enabled handtracking in Unity. I can now add that to my prototyping skillset.

Limited User Research

The remote nature and compressed timeframe of the Hackathon made direct user research difficult to integrate. For future Hackathons, I will build a project plan that explicitly includes user research cycles.

Remote VR Design/Development Challenge

Several aspects of VR design and development (particularly design brainstorming around intricate 3-D interaction) were challenging to implement due to the remote nature of the team. For future remote work, I will leverage 360 sketching and basic object placement in AFrame to create a remote meeting space for VR design work.

VR Technology Baselining

There was a disparity between team members in knowledge of VR technology basics. This limited the ability of some members to easily contribute to the product during the development stage. For future hackathon work, I will build a "VR Basics" Powerpoint that will help equalize the VR knowledge of the team.
