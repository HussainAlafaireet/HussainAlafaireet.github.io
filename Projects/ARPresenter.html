AR Presenter

AR Product Presentation for the Chaotic Trade Show Environment


Role: Product Designer  |  Duration: 8 Weeks
Tools Used: Unity, Hololens Toolkit, Microsoft Sharing Services
UX Skills Used: User/Stakeholder Interviews, Personas, Storyboarding, Prototyping , Stakeholder Pitching

Summary: AR Presenter, developed by Fast Effect in Summer 2018, is a Hololens-based Augmented Reality software used to demo products at trade shows. AR Presenter uses a careful combination of local-area location sharing, simplified presenter control, and modular storytelling to defeat the the problems typical AR products face when used in the show environment.

My Contributions: As the Product Designer, I was the guiding light behind the AR Presenter prototype. I conducted stakeholder research to determine user needs, generated the interaction design for AR Presenter, and oversaw it's development cycle. Along the way, I introduced Fast Effect to the Affinity Wall user-centered design technique, as well as fostering an enhanced degree of ownership and teamwork within an interdisciplinary development team.

Understanding The Needs Of The Trade Show

I interviewed client stakeholders to understand their needs and where previous trade show AR had failed. I analyzed the interviews with an affinity wall and personas, and from these, derived 4 design principles with which to drive the project:

Use only proven AR tracking technologies, and avoid room-wide spacial mapping:

Avoid as many assumptions about venue format as possible, and build adaptability into the design



Tailor the initial prototype to a specific client message

Build a modular storytelling approach into the app.

Creating The AR Presenter Design

Based on the design principles, I facilitated a design brainstorming session with the development team. This session lead to the concept of AR Presenter - a modular storytelling app that would use AR content to demonstrate products.

In AR Presenter, a central presenter at a trade show wears a Hololens unit, and gives a product demo presentation composed of AR content. This content can be viewed by nearby presentees (who are also wearing Hololenses) in real-time.

I took the base concept of AR Presenter, and determined the secondary features required to support the overall concept:

Adaptive Location Selection

The presenter has the option of placing a "marker" hologram to designate the physical location that best suits the presentation at hand.

Presenter Experience

The presenter can switch between pieces of AR content, and can trigger specific interactions on AR content, which are then reflected in the presentee view in real-time.

Voice Chat

AR Presenter implements voice chat through the Hololens microphone and speaker system to compensate for ambient noise.

Standalone Mode

Presentations can be run in a "standalone" mode, with presentees able to use simplified versions of the presenter controls to walk through the experience.

"Parts" Ecosystem

The presentation system is modular. The AR Presenteer app has a dedicated mode for building presentations, utilizing a library of AR content that can be expanded as needed.

"Safe" Shared Environment

AR Presenter utilizes a sharing experience routed through a wireless router packaged with the system. This ensures a usable shared experience even without internet access.

I then generated the interaction design for the app via a set of sketches and low fidelity mock-ups.

Building Out The Prototype

I pitched my design to the client, and got permission to build a prototype. I then designed a 2-week sprint for our development team. I also developed an extended user journey map to assist in the development process.

During the sprints, I acted as a Unity programmer, working on the shared experience and microphone functionality.

Outcome

At the end of the second week of sprints, we invited our client stakeholders in to test our demo. We walked the client through our demo scenario. We then encouraged them to experiment with the menu system, voice chat, and shared AR object control.

The client was pleased with the demo's capabilities, and authorized a paid contract for the full version of AR Presenter.

Reflection

Presenter/Presentee Functionality

The Shared Presenter/Presentee functionality clearly communicated the power and vision of AR Presenter's trade show experience.

Menu Design Offset and Sizing

The presentation layer's sizing, visual design, and positioning proved to be both usable and pleasing.

"Wow and Dazzle" Visual Fidelity

The client was impressed by the immersive factor of our demo scenario. AR Presenter proved its power to tell compelling narratives.


Attention Direction

The client had difficulty knowing precisely where in the road scenario they should be looking at a particular time. Future scenarios would need to be much more clear as to where the "action" was happening.

Left-Handed Presenter Controls

The client pointed out that left-handed users might be less comfortable with the right-positioned presentation controls. We would need to offer a left-aligned menu as an option.

"Scalable Holograms"

The client wanted to have the presentation holograms be scalable, so that they could be adapted to the presentation area in use.
