Life as A Gym

AR Exercise Support for the Busy College Lifestyle


View Process   View Final Output

Project Overview

How do we get University students to actually exercise regularly?

Roles: Team Lead, UX Designer, UX Researcher, AR Developer  |  Duration: 4 Months
Tools Used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Premiere, AFrame, AR.JS, Youtube Gaming
UX Skills Used: UX Design, UI Design, UX Research, Interviewing, AR/VR Prototyping, AR/VR Development, Concept Pitching

Summary: Life as a Gym (LG) is a set of Internet-connected AR glasses that help busy University students maintain exercise routines. LG scans the users environment during their daily routines, and identifies opportunities for on-the-spot exercise. LG coordinates its suggestions with the users schedule, seemlessly inserting exercise into the users lives.

My Contributions:

As Team Lead, I established the product vision and determined the timetable for all work assignments.

As UX Researcher, I helped develop and conduct all user research, with particular focus on the research question, diary study, and user enactments.

As UX Designer, I crafted the core concept of LG, including it's primary feature set.

As AR Developer, I developed the design and technical components of the prototype we would use to pitch our concept.

Process

I worked with three other UX professionals on Life as a Gym (LG): AR smart glasses to support exercise.

LG works by scanning the environment in the users vicinity, and finding opportunities for informal exercise routines. By marrying these exercise scans with an understanding of the users' schedule, LG makes finding the time and place for exercise effortless.

We had 4 months to design and create our concept prototype, with a demonstration session in front of stakeholders as the final target.


Brainstorming

The project's goal was to develop a smart technology solution for a health challenge that University students typically face.

Our first step was to pull on our collective experience as University students ourselves to brainstorm issues and potential solution areas.

We found two primary trends in our brainstorming:

  • Health Reminders: Reminding students to take time to maintain their health.
  • Exercise: Enabling exercise as a means of preventative care.

Our problem space therefore became exercise reminders for University students.

Research

We next developed a user research plan to better understand the challenges associated with exercise time management.

Research Questions

What factors motivate the target audience to exercise?

What barriers prevent target audience from exercising?

How does target audience learn how to exercise?

What kinds of exercises does our target audience actually do?

What resources does our target audience use to enable their exercise?

Study Design

To determine the answers to our research questions, we designed two complimentary studies:


Diary Study

Goals
  • Explore daily exercise behaviors
  • Discover novel barriers to exercise
Participants
  • 3 Participants for 6 days
  • Participants screened to vary type and intensity of exercise.
Instruments
  • Google Forms for Diary Capture
  • Twice a day text reminders

Survey

Goals
  • Determine most-used exercise
  • Determine must-used exercise resources
Participants
  • Participants recruited via multiple institution sub-reddits
  • 176 responses
Instruments
  • Google Survey Form

Research Findings

Lack of time was the primary barrier to exercise

  • Most prevalent and important finding.
  • Time was both primary exercise concern and primary exercise metric
    • Participants measured amount of exercise in time rather than effort.
    • Time cited as primary barrier to exercise completion.

Endurance and Strength exercises were the most prominent forms of exercise.

  • Running and biking were most common exercises
  • Weight training and floor exercises next most common.

Users often need guidance when doing exercises to learn how to do them correctly

  • Significant amount of discussion in both survey and diary study about exercise education needs.
  • Particularly apparent in the use of strength exercise, where proper form is particularly important, both for effectiveness, and to prevent injury.

Design

Revised Ideation

Based on our new primary design insights, we generated 3 new design concepts:

Smart Exercise Mat

  • Exercise Mat with built-in instructional screens and lights
  • Helps people learn proper form
  • Portable nature adds convenience

Smart Gesture Guide

  • Portable camera/sensor unit that observes users as they exercise, and provides guidance.
  • Built-in screen and speaker allows for multiple instruction methods

Life As a Gym

  • AR Glasses with ability to scan environment for on-the-spot exercise opportunities
  • Connected to users' calendar to ensure on-the-spot exercises fit within daily schedule

Final Design Selection

We selected the Life as a Gym concept for the following factors:

  • The "always on" nature of the glasses best integrated with the time management finding.
  • The lightweight nature of the product would integrate well with a busy student lifestyle.
  • The use of AR to enable exercise is still a relatively unexplored area.

User Enactments

To determine the full set of features LG should have, we decided to conduct User Enactments. This research method was particularly helpful given the emerging nature of AR Technology.

User Enactments Study Design

Goals
  • Test proposed LG features
  • Explore AR expectations and boundaries
Participants
  • 9 University students recruited via snowball sampling.
  • Enactments held at university facilities
Instruments
  • 7 Use Case Scenarios
  • Moderated sketch-based walkthroughs for each scenario
  • Post-testing debriefing session

User Enactment Findings

We conducted an affinity wall analysis to analyze our findings and convert them to a set of design principles. The full affinity wall can be viewed here.


Focus on individual usage scenarios

  • Users felt uncomfortable with involving their friends in LG activities. They felt that the glasses would take focus away from their friends.
  • Virtually all participants flat-out stated that they would not use the product in social settings.

Recommend walking and running as primary exercises, climbing as secondary

  • We found that participants were most comfortable with walking, running, or climbing as public activities.

UI design should be optimized to support the visual experience of walking/running

  • Users were willing to work with voice, text, and gesture controls in our study.
  • The amount of text used often impeded the user's sight, becoming a potential safety hazard.
  • We would reduce the text used to the minimum needed, and move text just outside of user's central view.

Provide multiple choices

  • Users preferred having moderate levels of choice, particularly in the realms of route selection and user profile preference.

Only track exercise-relevant data

  • Users had privacy concerns and did not want LG to access their friend lists, or contact information
  • Our design would only track user calories and exercises for user reference.

Provide calories, time, and health relevant information

  • Calories burned was the most requested health information.
  • Users wanted to track how much time a given exercise route would take, and how that fit with their schedule.

Provide enough guidance for onboarding and data usage

  • Users had very limited experience base for AR smartglass technology
  • LG onboarding would need to introduce base AR concepts, as well as clarify data usage.

System Proposal

Based on our findings, our final system proposal for LG consisted of the following refined feature set:

Exercise Computer Vision

LG's computer vision actively scans the environment for exercise opportunities (stairs, long straightaways, etc), and catalogs them for later use.


Time & Schedule Integration

LG integrates with your electronic calendar and knows when you need to be somewhere.


Navigation

LG acts as a personal navigation device with turn-by-turn guidance through any activities you perform.


Voice Assistance & Input

Users can hear LG glasses speak, and they can interact with the glasses using voice.


Exercise Moments

LG smartly manages your time, and recommends exercises that fit into your schedule.


Companion App

LG comes with a companion application that gives users more in-depth information about their daily exercise.

Reflect

First Person Video Mockups are a powerful way to convey AR Design

  • Both promo vid and livestream prototype communicated concept effectively.
  • Mockup methods generated prototype in short amounts of time.

Our User Enactments allowed us to discover edge cases

  • User enactments easily the most effective research methods used in our project.
  • Allowed us to isolate features and issues we would have never thought to address.

Intermingling design and research sessions informed both effectively

  • Early use of initial designs inform research design, which then fed back into the next design iteration.

Sketch-based AR prototyping remains a challenge

  • User Enactment sketches had only limited success in conveying LG concept. We had to augment them with verbal descriptions.
  • Holding sketches during enactments caused participants to focus on test facilitators, drawing attention away from the enactment.

Safety issues for LG use remain a concern

  • Discovered several potential safety concerns, including situational awareness, privacy concerns, and late-night street usage.
  • No testing done to determine if LG design sufficiently covered these concerns.

A physical AR glasses prototype proved infeasible

  • A prototype that utilized physical AR glasses would have better communicated our concept.
  • Technical challenges made physical glasses infeasible for demonstration timeline.
